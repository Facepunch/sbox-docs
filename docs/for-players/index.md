# For Players

Welcome to s&box! This section is for people who just want to play games created in s&box, not necessarily build them.

## Getting the Game

s&box acts as a platform for discovering and playing games made by the community. To get started, you must download the s&box client:
[Install s&box through Steam](steam://run/2129370)

Once installed, simply launch the game through your Steam library.

## How it Works

Unlike a single traditional game, s&box is a platform. When you connect to a server or launch a single-player experience, the engine dynamically downloads and mounts **Addons** (code, maps, and assets). You don't need to manually subscribe to items in a Workshop; everything is handled seamlessly in the background.

> **Roblox/Garry's Mod Equivalent:** If you are familiar with Roblox or Garry's Mod, s&box functions similarly from a player's perspective. It is a single executable that serves as a portal to thousands of distinct user-generated games and experiences. You launch the platform, browse for a game, and the engine automatically fetches the required files to play it.

## What You Will Do

As a player, your interactions with the platform generally follow these three patterns:

1. **Browsing and Joining Games:**
   When you launch s&box, the **Main Menu** serves as your hub. You can browse featured, popular, and newly released games. Clicking a game tile allows you to view its details, join a live server, or host your own session for friends.

2. **Managing Addons and Storage:**
   Because s&box automatically downloads content for every game you join, your disk usage can grow over time. You can manage your downloaded addons directly through the main menu settings, clearing out local caches for games you no longer play to free up space.

3. **Customizing Your Avatar:**
   You can customize your global in-game appearance using the **Avatar Editor** found in the main menu. You can equip hats, shirts, and accessories, and adjust your character's body shape and skin color. This saved avatar is used automatically in any game that supports the standard Citizen models.

## Troubleshooting & Errors

:::warning Common Issues

- **Missing Textures/Models (Pink/Black Checkers or ERROR signs):** This usually means the game you are playing has missing assets on the creator's end, or your download was interrupted. Try rejoining the server or verifying your s&box files in Steam.
- **Connection Failed/Timed Out:** The server might be down, or your connection might be blocked by a firewall. Ensure s&box is allowed through your Windows Firewall.
- **Game Crashes:** If a specific game crashes consistently, it might be a bug in that game's custom code, not the engine itself. Check if there are updates or report it to the game's creator if possible.
:::

- **Large Initial Downloads:** Keep in mind that when joining a heavily customized server for the first time, the engine must download all its unique addons. Your connection speed will dictate how quickly you can join. Subsequent joins will be nearly instantaneous because the files are cached locally.
- **Shader Compilation:** Upon loading a new game, you may experience brief stutters as the engine compiles shaders for the first time. This is normal and will smooth out as you continue to play and the shader cache builds up.

## Things to Keep in Mind

- **Game-Specific Overrides:** While you can customize your Avatar in the main menu, note that some custom games might override your appearance completely to fit their theme (e.g., forcing you to play as a specific character class or an inanimate object). This is normal behavior dictated by the game's developer.
- **Save Data Wipes:** Because many games are actively in development, their creators might wipe player progress or save data during major updates.
