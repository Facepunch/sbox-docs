# Motion Editing

Motion Editing lets you tweak animations, mix different motions together, and fine-tune movement generated by the Movie Maker.

### Creating a Motion Track

Most properties can be controlled via a Motion Track, especially properties related to movement (`WorldPosition`, `WorldRotation`, or Transform).

1. Select your target GameObject (e.g., a Camera).
2. Right-click the **Transform** property in the Movie Maker track list.
3. Select **Add Motion Track**.
4. The timeline will now show a Motion block.

### Trimming and Splitting

You can edit motion blocks directly on the timeline:

*   **Trim:** Drag the left or right edges of a motion block to shorten it. The underlying animation data is preserved but only the active region will play.
*   **Split:** Place the playhead (the vertical red line) where you want to cut, right-click the motion block, and select **Split**. This is useful for removing mistakes from recorded motions.

### Blending Motions

If you have two overlapping motion blocks on the same track, the engine will automatically attempt to blend between them over the overlapping duration. This is perfect for smoothing out a transition between a walking animation and a running animation, or blending two different camera paths.

1. Ensure two motion blocks exist on the same track.
2. Drag one block so that its edge slightly overlaps with the other block.
3. The overlapping area visually indicates a crossfade blend.

## Configuration

When selecting a motion block, the Inspector reveals its specific properties.

| Property | Description |
|:---|:---|
| `Start Time` | The exact second the motion block begins playing in the timeline. |
| `Duration` | The total length of the motion block. |
| `Speed` | A multiplier for playback speed (e.g., 2.0 plays the motion twice as fast). |
| `Loop Mode` | Defines what happens if the track extends past the block's content: `None`, `Loop`, or `PingPong`. |

## Troubleshooting

:::danger "My motion block isn't blending smoothly!"
Ensure that the properties within both overlapping motion blocks are compatible. Blending works best with continuous numerical data (like vectors or floats). Also, verify the overlap duration is long enough; a 0.1-second blend may appear as a sudden snap.
:::

:::warning "The character snaps back to the start when the motion ends!"
This happens because the Movie Maker no longer controls the property once the block ends, reverting to its original state or the next available keyframe. To fix this, ensure your `Loop Mode` is set to `Hold` or explicitly place a keyframe after the motion block to maintain the final position.
:::
